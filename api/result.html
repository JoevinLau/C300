Transport Activity

Transport Activity (in tonne-km) quantifies the transport output and is calculated according to the GLEC Framework as the freight mass multiplied by the distance travelled. The calculation method varies depending on the freight unit:

Tonnes:

Mass [tonnes] × Distance [km]

TEU/FEU Container:

Number of containers × Mass per container [tonnes] × Distance [km]

Pallets:

Number of pallets × Mass per pallet [tonnes] × Distance [km]

1 tonnes

Freight Unit

The freight unit defines the freight and has an impact on the calculation. The use of TEU/FEU units is recommended for shipping, as the calculation is based on the number of containers rather than the cargo weight in tonnes.

TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit) and FEU (Forty-foot Equivalent Unit) are international standard units of measurement used in container shipping to measure cargo capacity. One TEU corresponds to a 20-foot ISO container, whilst one FEU corresponds to a 40-foot ISO container (1 FEU = 2 TEU). They enable the precise planning of vessel capacities and port handling operations.
